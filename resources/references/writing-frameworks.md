# Cold Email Writing Frameworks

Named copywriting frameworks for B2B cold email, with structure, when to use,
and annotated examples.

## Framework Index

| Framework | Best For | Structure |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| PAS | Standard cold email | Problem > Agitate > Solve |
| AIDA | Multi-step sequences | Attention > Interest > Desire > Action |
| BAB | Outcome-focused | Before > After > Bridge |
| Short Trigger | Signal-based outbound | Signal Reference > Pain > Proof > Ask |
| Pattern Interrupt | Crowded inboxes | Unexpected opener > Relevance > Value > Ask |
| Do The Math | Quantifiable ROI | Stat > Implication > Proof > Ask |
| Question-Led | Discovery stage | Question > Context > Proof > Ask |

---

## PAS (Problem, Agitate, Solve)

**When to use:** Standard outbound when you have a clear pain hypothesis.

**Structure:**
1. Name a specific problem they have (10-15 words)
2. Amplify the cost of not fixing it (10-15 words)
3. Bridge to how the fix works (10-15 words)
4. CTA (5-10 words)

**Example:**
> Most outbound teams verify emails after the campaign launches.
> That means 3-5% bounce rates and domain reputation damage by week two.
> [proof_company] added a pre-send verification loop that keeps bounces under 0.5%.
> Worth a look?

---

## BAB (Before, After, Bridge)

**When to use:** When you can paint a clear before/after for the prospect.

**Structure:**
1. Describe their current state (10-15 words)
2. Describe the desired state (10-15 words)
3. Bridge: how to get there (10-15 words)
4. CTA (5-10 words)

**Example:**
> Right now your SDR team is manually researching prospects before every call.
> After: 80% of that research is pre-loaded into the CRM with enrichment automations.
> [proof_company] set it up in a week with Clay + HubSpot.
> Want to see how they structured it?

## Short Trigger

**When to use:** Signal-based outbound where timing matters more than persuasion.

**Structure:**
1. Reference the signal directly (5-10 words)
2. Connect to a plausible pain (10-15 words)
3. One proof point (10-15 words)
4. CTA (5-10 words)

**Example:**
> Saw the VP Sales posting at [company].
> Teams hiring for this role usually hit outbound infrastructure gaps before the new hire ramps.
> [proof_company] locked their stack down in two weeks before their new VP started.
> Short version of how they did it?

---

## Pattern Interrupt

**When to use:** When prospects are oversaturated with standard cold email patterns.

**Structure:**
1. Unexpected or contrarian opener (5-15 words)
2. Quick relevance bridge (10-15 words)
3. Proof or specific value (10-15 words)
4. CTA (5-10 words)

**Example:**
> You probably get 10 emails a week about outbound tools.
> This one is about the infrastructure mistake that makes all of them underperform.
> [proof_company] fixed it and their reply rates went from 2% to 18%.
> 2-minute breakdown. Want it?

---

## Do The Math

**When to use:** When the value proposition is quantifiable and the prospect is analytical.

**Structure:**
1. One specific stat or number (5-10 words)
2. What that number implies for their business (10-20 words)
3. How a peer changed that number (10-15 words)
4. CTA (5-10 words)

**Example:**
> Your team sends about 1,000 cold emails a week.
> At 22% annual email decay, 220 of those addresses will be dead within 12 months.
> [proof_company] runs a quarterly verification cycle that keeps bounces at 0.3%.
> Worth comparing to your current setup?

---

## Question-Led

**When to use:** Discovery-stage outreach or when you want to provoke thought.

**Structure:**
1. Ask a question they cannot easily dismiss (10-20 words)
2. Provide brief context on why the question matters (10-15 words)
3. One proof point (10-15 words)
4. CTA (5-10 words)

**Example:**
> How do you decide which inbound leads go to an AE vs. nurture?
> Teams without a scoring system usually route 40% of leads to the wrong path.
> [proof_company] built a 3-tier scoring model that cut misroutes by 85%.
> Want to see their criteria?

## Framework Selection Guide

| Scenario | Best Framework |
|----------|---------------|
| Clear pain hypothesis + strong proof | PAS |
| Quantifiable before/after | BAB |
| Signal-based timing play | Short Trigger |
| Crowded inbox / oversaturated ICP | Pattern Interrupt |
| Analytical buyer + clear ROI | Do The Math |
| Buyer in early discovery | Question-Led |
| Executive / VP audience | Short Trigger or Do The Math |
| Manager / IC audience | PAS or Pattern Interrupt |
